$$ U = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\theta} \end{pmatrix} $$

For eigenstate \( |1\rangle \), we have \( U |1\rangle = e^{i\theta} |1\rangle \). In the ideal case, the phase \( \theta \) is given by:

$$ \theta = \frac{2 \pi k}{2^n} $$

Hamiltonian QPE Analysis

We aim to estimate the eigenvalues of a Hermitian matrix \( H \) using QPE. Since \( H \) is Hermitian, \( e^{iHt} \) is unitary, allowing us to apply QPE to it.

For an eigenvector \( | \lambda \rangle \) of \( H \) with eigenvalue \( \lambda \):

$$ e^{iHt} | \lambda \rangle = e^{i\lambda t} | \lambda \rangle $$

However, QPE outputs the phase \( \varphi \), so we rewrite as:

$$ e^{i\lambda t} | \lambda \rangle = e^{2 \pi i \varphi} | \lambda \rangle $$

Where:

$$ \varphi = \frac{\lambda t}{2 \pi} \implies \lambda = \frac{2 \pi \varphi}{t} $$

In practice, we estimate \( \varphi \) as \( \tilde{\varphi} = \frac{m}{2^t} \), where \( m \) is the best \( t \)-bit approximation to \( \varphi \).
